Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about ETUDE

Who is eligible to apply for the program?

High school students (grades 9-12) who are highly motivated and interested in research are eligible to apply. We look for students with strong academic records and a genuine passion for scientific inquiry.

What is the program fee and what does it cover?

The program fee covers the research grant provided directly to the hosting lab, program administration, matching services, and ongoing support throughout the research experience. Specific pricing varies by program length and project type. Please contact us for detailed information.

Can I work remotely or do I need to be on campus?

Both options are available depending on the project and lab requirements. Many projects can be conducted remotely, while others may require in-person collaboration. We work with you to find the best match for your situation.

What kind of research projects are available?

Projects span a wide range of disciplines including biology, chemistry, physics, computer science, engineering, social sciences, and more. We match students with projects that align with their interests and academic goals.

How long does the program last?

Programs typically run for one semester (Fall or Spring), with options for extended engagement. The exact duration depends on the project scope and your availability.

What do I receive at the end of the program?

Upon successful completion, you'll receive a project portfolio documenting your research contributions, a letter of recommendation from your mentor, and a certificate of completion. These materials are designed to strengthen your college applications.

For Labs: How much funding can we receive?

Funding amounts vary based on project scope and duration. Grants typically range from $2,000 to $10,000 per student match. Funds are provided directly to the lab with no restrictions on use—they can cover equipment, materials, travel, or other research expenses.

For Labs: What are our responsibilities as mentors?

Mentors provide guidance, set project expectations, and offer regular check-ins with students. The time commitment is typically 2-4 hours per week. We handle all administrative aspects, so you can focus on research and mentorship.

How are students matched with labs?

We use a comprehensive matching process that considers student interests, academic background, lab requirements, and project availability. Our goal is to create mutually beneficial partnerships that advance both research and student development.

Can international students apply?

Yes, international students are welcome to apply. However, for in-person programs, students must be able to obtain appropriate visas and travel to the US. Remote options are available for international students.
